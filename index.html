    Scam Prevention Tips

    Stay informed and protect yourself from online threats

    Be Wary of Urgency

    Scammers create false urgency to pressure you into acting quickly. Legitimate organizations give you time to think.

    Verify Links

    Always hover over links before clicking. Check the actual URL destination. Be suspicious of shortened URLs or misspelled domains.

    Never Share OTPs

    One-Time Passwords are for your eyes only. No legitimate company will ever ask you to share your OTP.

    Verify Sender Identity

    If you receive suspicious communications, contact the organization directly using official contact information, not details from the message.

    Too Good to Be True

    Free prizes, lottery winnings, and inheritance from unknown sources are classic scam tactics. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

    Protect Personal Info

    Never provide sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or social security numbers via email or text.

    Check Email Addresses

    Scammers use email addresses that look similar to legitimate ones. Carefully check the sender's email domain.

    Look for Red Flags

    Poor grammar, spelling errors, and unprofessional formatting are common signs of phishing attempts.
